Illinois Revenue Alliance Meeting notes

6/25/2025

Legislative debrief

3 legislative wins ($355M)

  • Corporate offshore sheltered income (GILTI) ($200M)
  • 80/20 Rule ($80M)
  • Changes ED20 Rule - If an Illinois corporation kept 80% of their profit off shore they didn’t have to pay tax. Now they have to pay a tax.
  • Joyce to Finagan ($75M)

Digital Ad tax ($150M) - Popular across chambers. - It was included in the senate but removed before the final bill passed to the senate, house moderates made clear that they will not vote for a budget that include it. - Worry from legislators about being subject to litigation. They point to Maryland. CP note: Any new tax passed will be subject to lawsuits at least in Chicago, it’s like a law of public finance.

Shift in overton window - taxing rich becoming more popular.

Thoughts on governor’s role CP note: speak’s to CTU’s revenue campaign - Patrick Brosnon - Governor did not want Digital Ad tax - Governor promoting austerity discipline to speak to anti-tax culture (i.e. voters). -Hannah Gelder - ILRA didn’t target governor.
